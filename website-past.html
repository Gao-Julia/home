Did you know?

Around 50 million years ago, whales were actually 4-legged creatures named “Pakicetus'' that walked on land. they were actually classified as ungulates. This group includes sheep, antelopes and pigs. As a matter of fact, the closest living relative to whales is the hippopotamus.

However most of their resources were in the water, therefore their bodies evolved over millions of years over time to adapt to the ocean. Once they were in the water, the weight of gravity didn’t affect the weight of their bones allowing them to grow ginormously large.

How do we know this?

Most blue whales have vestigial bone evolutionary remnants from when their ancestors. For example a lot of them had leftover hind bones.

The first baleen whales appeared 27.5 million years ago. These whales were only 18 feet long, making it easy for predators such as giant sharks to prey on them . It wasn't until the ice age (3 million years ago) where the oceans grew colder and more plankton and krill started to show up, providing huge amounts of nutrition for whales allowing them to grow.

1900s Whale baleen fashion

Inaccuratly recorded as whale bone, whale baleen was a huge part of european fashion. Baleen is made of keratin (the same strong material found in fingernails). Thus making it an ideal choice for structuring clothes. Such as hat brims, collars, hooped frames, skirts and most importantly, corsets.

However, due to the high commercial of whale baleen and other body parts, blue whales were almost driven to extinction. thankfully, whaling was banned in 1996.

Whale oil

Another super sought out whale part was whale blubber. This is because when whale blubber is melted and rendered it turns into a waxy substance called whale oil.Whale oil was a primary ingredient in soap, margarine, and oil-burning lamps. Today, some indigenous Arctic communities, such as the Inuit, still harvest blubber and render it for use in traditional whale-oil lamps.
